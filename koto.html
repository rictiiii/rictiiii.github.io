Overview

Modernizing Vehicle Ownership

Koto is a comprehensive automotive service platform created for Toyota in Japan to modernize the vehicle ownership experience. The app combines two critical features: an online shop for automotive services and products, and a digital key system that enables contactless vehicle access and control.

Developed specifically for the Japanese market, the platform serves Toyota vehicle owners who want convenient access to automotive services while eliminating the friction of traditional physical keys and in-person service scheduling.

Objective

Integrated Service Ecosystem

Design an integrated automotive service ecosystem that:

  • Simplifies service discovery: Enable users to easily browse, compare, and purchase automotive services and products
  • Streamlines appointment booking: Reduce friction in scheduling maintenance and service appointments
  • Modernizes vehicle access: Provide secure, contactless digital key functionality for enhanced convenience
  • Creates unified experience: Seamlessly integrate e-commerce and connected car features into cohesive platform
  • Supports multi-channel access: Design for both native mobile app and responsive web experiences

Research & Discovery

Understanding User Pain Points

Research Approach

I conducted comprehensive research to understand how vehicle owners currently manage automotive services and what prevents them from using digital solutions:

  • User interviews: Spoke with 20 vehicle owners across different demographics and vehicle types
  • Service provider interviews: Interviewed automotive service centers to understand operational constraints
  • Competitive analysis: Evaluated existing solutions like Tesla app, Openbay, and YourMechanic
  • Behavioral analysis: Studied how users currently schedule services and what friction points exist

Key Pain Points Discovered

1. Price Opacity Creates Distrust

Hidden fees were the #1 complaint about traditional automotive service. Users wanted to see complete pricing before committing to a service appointment. Many avoided digital booking because they feared surprise charges.

2. Scheduling Friction Leads to Delays

Research revealed that 67% of users still preferred in-person scheduling—not because they wanted to visit in person, but because phone calls were frustrating and existing digital solutions were clunky. The average booking took 15 minutes across multiple phone calls and confirmation emails.

3. Physical Key Management is Inconvenient

Users struggled with dropping off keys for service appointments, coordinating pickup times, and sharing access with family members. Service centers also cited key management as a significant operational burden.

4. No Service History or Recommendations

Vehicle owners wanted personalized service recommendations based on their mileage and time intervals, but this information was rarely available. They had to remember maintenance schedules manually or rely on reminder stickers.

Design Process

From Research to Solution

Information Architecture & User Flows

Based on the research insights, I mapped out comprehensive user journeys focusing on the mobile app experience:

  • Created detailed flow diagrams for service discovery and booking
  • Designed onboarding survey to balance data collection with user patience
  • Prototyped checkout flow optimizations through iterative wireframing
  • Mapped digital key sharing scenarios (family, valet, service center) to identify edge cases
Comprehensive user flows mapping different entry points and authentication states through the booking experience
Early wireframe sketches exploring layout options and information hierarchy for key screens

Visual Design & Prototyping

I established a design system optimized for automotive context:

  • Dark theme interface: Matches automotive interior lighting and reduces glare for in-car usage
  • High-contrast UI: Ensures readability in bright outdoor environments (parking lots)
  • Large touch targets: Optimized for one-handed operation and winter glove usage
  • Component library: Reusable patterns for service cards, appointment slots, and booking flows

Testing & Iteration

Rigorous testing ensured both features met user needs and technical requirements:

  • Usability testing: Conducted moderated sessions with 12 participants across age groups
  • Field testing: Digital key functionality tested in real-world scenarios (parking lots, valets, service centers)
  • A/B testing: Tested checkout flow variations to optimize conversion rates
  • Beta program: Ran 8-week beta with 100 users and 5 partnered service centers

Key Iterations:

  • Simplified onboarding survey from 8 questions to 4 after observing drop-off
  • Added price comparison feature after beta users requested ability to evaluate service costs
  • Implemented proximity-based auto-unlock after field testing showed tap-to-unlock was too slow
  • Redesigned service cards to emphasize availability and pricing (initially too text-heavy)

Key Learnings

What I Learned

Hybrid Architecture Enables Best of Both Worlds

The decision to use native app for core features (digital key, notifications) while embedding web views for e-commerce provided optimal performance and development flexibility. Native features felt fast and integrated, while web content allowed rapid iteration without app store submission delays. This architectural choice reduced development time by 40% compared to pure native approach.

Transparency Builds Trust in Automotive Services

Clear, upfront pricing was the single most impactful factor in driving service bookings. Users consistently mentioned price transparency as their favorite feature. This taught me that in traditionally opaque industries (automotive, healthcare, legal), showing pricing before commitment can be a massive competitive advantage.

Digital Key Security Must Be Visible

Initial designs treated digital key functionality as "just working" without explaining security measures. User testing revealed significant hesitation to use the feature without understanding encryption, authentication, and backup procedures. Adding a one-time security explainer during first key setup increased adoption by 35%. The lesson: for trust-critical features, transparency about how security works is essential.

Service Integration Creates Sticky Experiences

Users who utilized both the shop and digital key features had 3x higher retention than those using only one feature. The integration between booking a service and automatically granting digital key access to the service center created a seamless experience that felt magical. This validated that connected ecosystems create more value than isolated features.

Automotive Context Requires Design Considerations

Designing for in-car and parking lot usage required different thinking than typical mobile apps. High-contrast UI for outdoor visibility, large touch targets for winter gloves, and proximity-based unlock for hands full of groceries were all contextual considerations that emerged from field testing. This taught me to always consider the physical environment where the product will be used.

Reflection

Koto represents a successful integration of e-commerce and connected car technology into a unified automotive ownership platform. By focusing on real user pain points—opaque pricing, inconvenient scheduling, physical key management—we created an experience that meaningfully improved how people interact with automotive services.

The success metrics—45% increase in bookings, 82% digital key adoption, 3.5x faster checkout—validate that when you reduce friction in traditionally cumbersome processes (service scheduling, vehicle access), users eagerly adopt digital solutions.

What I'm most proud of is the seamless integration between e-commerce and digital key functionality. Rather than feeling like two separate features, they work together cohesively—booking a service automatically handles key sharing, appointments appear in the calendar alongside key access permissions, and service history informs future recommendations.

Future Opportunities

If I were to continue evolving Koto, I'd focus on three key areas:

  • Predictive maintenance alerts: Leverage vehicle diagnostic data to proactively recommend services before issues become critical
  • Service provider marketplace: Open the platform to third-party service providers with quality ratings and competitive bidding
  • Fleet management features: Extend the platform to support multi-vehicle households and small business fleets
  • Insurance integration: Connect service history with insurance providers to offer usage-based discounts for well-maintained vehicles
