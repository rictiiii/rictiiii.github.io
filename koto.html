Overview

Modernizing Vehicle Ownership

Koto is a comprehensive automotive service platform designed to modernize the vehicle ownership experience. The app combines two critical features: an online shop for automotive services and products, and a digital key system that enables contactless vehicle access and control.

The platform serves vehicle owners who want convenient access to automotive services while eliminating the friction of traditional physical keys and in-person service scheduling.

Objective

Integrated Service Ecosystem

Design an integrated automotive service ecosystem that:

  • Simplifies service discovery: Enable users to easily browse, compare, and purchase automotive services and products
  • Streamlines appointment booking: Reduce friction in scheduling maintenance and service appointments
  • Modernizes vehicle access: Provide secure, contactless digital key functionality for enhanced convenience
  • Creates unified experience: Seamlessly integrate e-commerce and connected car features into cohesive platform
  • Supports multi-channel access: Design for both native mobile app and responsive web experiences
The Challenge

Breaking Down User Friction

Through user research with vehicle owners and analysis of existing automotive service platforms, I identified key pain points:

‚ùå Fragmented Service Experience

Vehicle owners had to use multiple apps and websites to schedule maintenance, purchase products, and manage vehicle features. This created friction and reduced engagement with automotive services.

‚ùå Complex Booking Flows

Traditional service scheduling required phone calls, multiple form submissions, and back-and-forth communication. Users wanted instant booking with transparent pricing and availability.

‚ùå Physical Key Limitations

Users struggled with managing physical keys‚Äîlosing them, sharing with family members, or needing spare keys for service appointments. They wanted modern, digital solutions.

‚ùå Lack of Service Transparency

Unclear pricing, hidden fees, and uncertainty about service quality made users hesitant to book automotive services through digital platforms.

Information Architecture

Dual-Path Platform Design

I designed a dual-path information architecture that supports both e-commerce flows and connected vehicle features while maintaining clear separation between these two distinct use cases.

Architecture Overview:

Native App Flow (Red Border)

The native mobile app provides quick access to essential features:

  • Login & Onboarding Survey: Personalized setup to understand user needs and vehicle details
  • Inbox: Notifications about service appointments, order updates, and vehicle status
  • Calendar: Upcoming appointments with ability to reschedule or modify services

Web View Flow (Green Border)

The embedded web experience powers the comprehensive e-commerce platform:

  • Service Search & Discovery: Browse automotive services with filtering and search capabilities
  • Shopping Cart: Add multiple services or products with transparent pricing
  • Service Detail & Booking: View comprehensive information and schedule appointments
  • Checkout Flow: Streamlined payment with saved methods and order confirmation
  • Order Management: Track current and past orders with detailed status updates

Key Architectural Decisions:

  • Hybrid approach: Native app for performance-critical features (digital key, calendar) + web view for content-rich e-commerce
  • Progressive disclosure: Show only relevant actions at each step to reduce cognitive load
  • Persistent navigation: Settings, favorites, and account access available throughout the experience
  • Cross-platform continuity: Seamless handoff between mobile app and web experience
Key Features

Core Platform Capabilities

Onboarding Survey

Upon first launch, users complete a brief survey that captures vehicle information, service preferences, and typical maintenance needs. This enables personalized service recommendations and pre-fills vehicle details for faster checkout.

Implementation:

  • Quick 3-4 question survey that doesn't feel overwhelming
  • Vehicle VIN scanning to auto-populate make, model, year
  • Service preference selection (convenience vs. cost, preferred times, location)
  • Optional integration with vehicle maintenance history

Service Marketplace

A comprehensive online shop where users can browse automotive services and products with transparent pricing, availability, and user reviews.

Service Categories:

  • Maintenance services: Oil changes, tire rotations, brake inspections
  • Repair services: Diagnostics, part replacements, body work
  • Enhancement services: Detailing, window tinting, accessories installation
  • Products: Parts, accessories, cleaning supplies

Discovery Features:

  • Smart search with vehicle-specific filtering
  • Service bundling suggestions (e.g., oil change + tire rotation)
  • Price comparison across service providers
  • Availability calendar with real-time scheduling
  • Service provider ratings and reviews

Streamlined Checkout

Optimized booking and payment flow designed to reduce cart abandonment and accelerate service scheduling.

Checkout Optimizations:

  • Saved payment methods: One-tap purchasing with secure stored cards
  • Appointment pre-selection: Available time slots shown before checkout
  • Vehicle auto-fill: Pre-populated vehicle details from onboarding survey
  • Order summary: Clear breakdown of services, fees, and total cost
  • Confirmation & calendar sync: Automatic appointment added to device calendar

Impact:

The streamlined checkout flow achieved 3.5x faster completion time compared to traditional phone-based booking, reducing average booking time from 15 minutes to under 5 minutes.

Digital Key Technology

Contactless vehicle access via smartphone, eliminating the need for physical keys in most scenarios.

Core Functionality:

  • Unlock/Lock: Tap-to-unlock or automatic proximity unlocking
  • Remote start: Pre-condition vehicle temperature before approaching
  • Key sharing: Grant temporary or permanent access to family members, valets, or service technicians
  • Security features: Biometric authentication, automatic lock when walking away

Service Integration:

When booking a service appointment, users can grant time-limited digital key access to service centers, allowing technicians to retrieve and return vehicles without physical key handoff. This feature achieved 82% adoption among users who had completed at least one service appointment.

Appointment Management

Integrated calendar showing upcoming services with ability to reschedule, modify, or cancel appointments.

  • Visual timeline of service history and upcoming appointments
  • Push notifications for appointment reminders, service completion, and vehicle ready for pickup
  • Real-time status updates during service (checked in, in progress, quality inspection, ready)
  • Service photo documentation (before/after for detailing, replaced parts)

Order Tracking & History

Comprehensive view of past and current orders with detailed service records.

  • Service receipts and invoices with itemized breakdowns
  • Maintenance history for warranty and resale documentation
  • Reorder functionality for recurring services
  • Service recommendation engine based on mileage and time intervals
Design Process

From Research to Launch

Phase 1: Research & Discovery

I conducted comprehensive research to understand user needs and competitive landscape:

  • User interviews: Spoke with 20 vehicle owners across different demographics and vehicle types
  • Service provider interviews: Interviewed automotive service centers to understand operational constraints
  • Competitive analysis: Evaluated existing solutions like Tesla app, Mileiq, Openbay, and YourMechanic
  • Technical research: Investigated digital key standards (Apple CarKey, Android Digital Key)

Key Research Insights:

  • Users wanted price transparency before committing to services‚Äîhidden fees were the #1 complaint about traditional shops
  • Convenience was valued over minor cost differences‚Äîusers would pay 10-15% more for easier booking
  • Digital key adoption was hindered by security concerns‚Äîneeded clear communication about encryption and authentication
  • Service recommendations based on mileage/time were highly valued but rarely available in existing platforms

Phase 2: Information Architecture & User Flows

I mapped out comprehensive user journeys for both primary features:

  • Created detailed flow diagrams distinguishing native app features from web-based e-commerce
  • Designed onboarding survey to balance data collection with user patience
  • Prototyped checkout flow optimizations through iterative wireframing
  • Mapped digital key sharing scenarios (family, valet, service center) to identify edge cases

Phase 3: Visual Design & Prototyping

I established a design system optimized for automotive context:

  • Dark theme interface: Matches automotive interior lighting and reduces glare for in-car usage
  • High-contrast UI: Ensures readability in bright outdoor environments (parking lots)
  • Large touch targets: Optimized for one-handed operation and winter glove usage
  • Component library: Reusable patterns for service cards, appointment slots, and booking flows
  • Responsive web design: Ensured e-commerce experience works across desktop, tablet, and mobile web

Phase 4: Testing & Iteration

Rigorous testing ensured both features met user needs and technical requirements:

  • Usability testing: Conducted moderated sessions with 12 participants across age groups
  • Field testing: Digital key functionality tested in real-world scenarios (parking lots, valets, service centers)
  • A/B testing: Tested checkout flow variations to optimize conversion rates
  • Beta program: Ran 8-week beta with 100 users and 5 partnered service centers

Key Iterations:

  • Simplified onboarding survey from 8 questions to 4 after observing drop-off
  • Added price comparison feature after beta users requested ability to evaluate service costs
  • Implemented proximity-based auto-unlock after field testing showed tap-to-unlock was too slow
  • Redesigned service cards to emphasize availability and pricing (initially too text-heavy)

Key Learnings

What I Learned

Hybrid Architecture Enables Best of Both Worlds

The decision to use native app for core features (digital key, notifications) while embedding web views for e-commerce provided optimal performance and development flexibility. Native features felt fast and integrated, while web content allowed rapid iteration without app store submission delays. This architectural choice reduced development time by 40% compared to pure native approach.

Transparency Builds Trust in Automotive Services

Clear, upfront pricing was the single most impactful factor in driving service bookings. Users consistently mentioned price transparency as their favorite feature. This taught me that in traditionally opaque industries (automotive, healthcare, legal), showing pricing before commitment can be a massive competitive advantage.

Digital Key Security Must Be Visible

Initial designs treated digital key functionality as "just working" without explaining security measures. User testing revealed significant hesitation to use the feature without understanding encryption, authentication, and backup procedures. Adding a one-time security explainer during first key setup increased adoption by 35%. The lesson: for trust-critical features, transparency about how security works is essential.

Service Integration Creates Sticky Experiences

Users who utilized both the shop and digital key features had 3x higher retention than those using only one feature. The integration between booking a service and automatically granting digital key access to the service center created a seamless experience that felt magical. This validated that connected ecosystems create more value than isolated features.

Automotive Context Requires Design Considerations

Designing for in-car and parking lot usage required different thinking than typical mobile apps. High-contrast UI for outdoor visibility, large touch targets for winter gloves, and proximity-based unlock for hands full of groceries were all contextual considerations that emerged from field testing. This taught me to always consider the physical environment where the product will be used.
